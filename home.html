Skin Type Test

When it comes to beauty, the only way to see success is to use products formulated for your specific skin type. Skincare products aren’t one-size-fits-all and it’s impossible to treat your skin properly if you don’t even know what you’re working with to start off.

Taking the time to learn your skin’s specific needs will help you not only choose the right items to balance skin but develop a healthier, more radiant complexion. And that’s the ultimate goal here, are we right?

First, let’s break down all the different skin types, shall we?

Normal

This skin type shows no oil production or flaky skin. The skin is smooth and soft to the touch (note: this is also the rarest skin type).

Oily

Your skin gets greasy or shiny during the day and you may notice large pores

Dry

With this skin type, your skin may feel tight or show dead skin flakes throughout the day.

Combination

This one gets a little tricky. You can see a little normal, a little dry AND a little oily. But a surefire way to tell if you have combination skin is if the skin is oily in the T-zone area and dry everywhere else.

Determining your skin

Blotting Method

From normal to dry and everything in between, here’s how you can determine your skin type:

First thing's first; you need to cleanse your skin of all dirt, oil and makeup to reveal your bare, beautiful and natural face. Use a mild and gentle cleanser, rinse with lukewarm water and pat your face dry.

Wait an hour to allow your skin to return to its natural state, then dab gently with a blotting paper. If you don't have blotting paper, you can separate the layers of a tissue until you are left with just one layer, this works pretty much the same as blotting paper.

Test Results

The amount of oil on the sheet will determine what type of skin you have. Now, let’s take a look at the results…

Dry Skin

If you find very little to no oil on the blotting sheet, your skin type is most likely dry. Also, take note of how your skin feels and looks; dry skin feels tight and stretched, and you may notice flaking around certain areas. Dry skin is more susceptible to the signs of aging, so using the right products are essential.
